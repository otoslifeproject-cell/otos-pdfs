The clinical anchor

The diagnosis is the thesis, in clinical language.

In April 2026 I received a diagnostic letter from Dr Fagbemi, consultant psychiatrist at Psymplicity, addressed to the CPFT Adult ADHD Team. It is medical correspondence about me — not an endorsement of anything I am building. But it names, in one sentence, the exact pattern you watch walk through your doors every week:

From the diagnostic letter · Psymplicity · April 2026
“Adult ADHD, previously complicated with excess alcohol use to self-medicate inadequately managed ADHD symptoms.”

Dr Fagbemi, Consultant Psychiatrist · addressed to the CPFT Adult ADHD Team.

Read the order of that sentence carefully.

Not “ADHD plus alcohol use disorder, treated separately.”

Not “alcohol use disorder, with ADHD as comorbidity.”

Adult ADHD, complicated by self-medication.

The order of operations is everything, and you have felt it in the rooms. Sitting in recovery meetings, I started to think something I could not say out loud: at least half the people here are undiagnosed ADHD. I still believe that. I suspect you have thought it too.

This letter is about my care — it is not proof that OTOS works. It is proof that the mechanism you see every week now has a name on letterhead. The driver underneath the addiction is real, documented, and treatable.

What you can see that others can't

The relapse the treatment system created itself.

Not your fault, and not your workload — this is about how the system was designed, never about the care you gave.

You see this cohort before anyone else does. You see them cycle — relapse, silence, return — and you carry the frustration of watching it happen with nowhere upstream to send them. I lived the sharpest version of that, inside dual-diagnosis treatment.

Recovery-sideWhere the pattern is seen first.
Illustrative · OTOS mock-up

On arrival at a residential rehab — funded by CGL and a charity, staffed by remarkable people doing real care — my ADHD medication was removed because it was a stimulant. The underlying condition was switched off by the treatment system itself. I came out fully twelve-stepped and addiction-informed, with nothing underneath holding the brain that started all of it. The relapse that followed was not a treatment failure. It was a system-generated relapse.

“The programme called it a character defect. I knew it wasn't.” — and the clinical letter, years later, proved it wasn't.

When ADHD is left waiting and services run on rigid criteria, people do desperate things to qualify for help. That is a design problem, not a moral failing — and it is the gap OTOS is built to close. The most honest line in my whole story was said by an alcohol liaison nurse: “You're right. There's nowhere for me to send you. I'd appreciate more places to signpost.”

You are not the problem in this story — you are the people most likely to see the missed driver first. What is missing is not your effort or your care. It is a thread that survives the handoff between your service and ADHD care.

Meeting in the middle

What I'm actually asking of you.

OTOS Continuity is a non-clinical layer that holds the thread between services. It does not diagnose, prescribe, treat or replace anyone. It surfaces signals; your team makes every decision. Nothing changes in how you work — what changes is what becomes visible.

OTOS elementWhat it doesWhy it matters to you
Micro check-insSimple signals between appointmentsShows whether someone is still connected, or starting to drift
Transition receiptsRecords whether a handoff happenedStops referrals to ADHD care disappearing into silence
Drift visibilityFlags missing follow-throughMakes relapse risk visible earlier, for a keyworker to act
Human review queueKeeps decisions with peopleNo automated clinical decisions — your judgement stays central
Tokenised continuity trailHolds minimal continuity signalsPathway visibility without becoming a new clinical record

Why CGL & CRS being in the room matters.

  • You see this cohort first — often long before ADHD services do.
  • You know who is drifting, who is engaging, and who is one missed appointment from crisis.
  • Through a put-forward model, you can refer — and OTOS never needs to see the data first.
