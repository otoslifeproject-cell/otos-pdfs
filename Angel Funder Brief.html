The problem · the founder

The gap between ADHD and addiction
is where people get lost.

A large share of adults with untreated or under-treated ADHD self-medicate. Many find their way into addiction services. Few get the ADHD half of the picture identified, assessed or treated in any timely way. The system keeps treating the addiction crisis, but not always the underlying condition driving it. The gap between “you're on the list” and “you're being held” is where drift, relapse, repeat presentation and devastation happen. We are already paying for this failure — just in the worst possible places.

The mechanism

OTOS Continuity is the wedge,
not the cure.

Continuity is the infrastructure that makes the intervention hold. It does one thing well: it holds the line between the moments a clinical service can hold. Not a treatment, not a diagnosis, not a prescriber.

Nothing changes in how partner teams work. What changes is what they can see. OTOS surfaces signals — the partner teams make the decisions.

What OTOS Continuity is designed to do.

  • Micro check-ins with the participant, at human cadence.
  • RCE-style courses on ADHD, addiction and recovery literacy.
  • Appointment reminders, ahead of and after the moments that usually slip.
  • CGL or keyworker support messages, where lawful and explicitly agreed.
  • Continuity prompts when the system has gone quiet on someone.
  • Human review of any signal that needs a human.
  • Drift and silence visibility — the absence of contact becomes a signal.
  • Engagement tracking with consent.
  • Full audit trail and a learning report at the end.

What OTOS will not pretend to be.

  • Not a diagnostic service.
  • Not a prescribing service.
  • Not an addiction treatment.
  • Not a replacement for NHS, CGL, CPFT, CRS, GPs, psychiatrists, recovery services or crisis support.
  • Not clinically proven yet.
  • Not endorsed by any partner unless that partner is explicitly named on the day.
Qualified clinical partners would be required for any diagnosis, prescribing or medication route. OTOS provides continuity around clinical work. It does not perform it.

Route 02

Direct-to-individual continuity for adults already on or off waiting lists.

Route 03

Route 04

Underwriting the demonstrator so it can stand on independent footing.

Route 05

Campaign / movement

OTOS Life as a public mission, with continuity as the mechanism people can feel.

Route 06

Hybrid

A combination of the above, shaped to whichever doors open first.

Flagship demonstrator · candidate hypothesis

The flagship ADHD continuity demonstrator.

This is not a new clinical service. It is a tightly bounded demonstrator to find the cohort, hold the thread and test whether earlier ADHD recognition changes the addiction and recovery pathway. A small CGL-linked or addiction-service-linked cohort would be identified, routed into appropriate ADHD assessment, diagnosis and medication support through qualified clinical channels, wrapped in OTOS Continuity for 12 weeks, and measured for candidate changes. Small. Surgical. Enough to prove or stop.

Candidate measures · addictive behaviours · relapse indicators · CGL attendance · ADHD appointment attendance · engagement with support · RCE course engagement · A&E presentations · crisis / drift signals · recovery progress · participant-reported stability.

Candidate demonstrator hypothesis, not a proven outcome. The point is to learn, not to claim.

The honest gate · the precedent

Not dependent on a single
NHS door opening.

CGL and the ADHD Team may or may not be permitted to share enough data or pathway knowledge to identify a cohort jointly. If that gate stays closed, four lawful parallel routes are ready — not plan-Bs.

  • CGL put-forward model — the service refers; OTOS never sees the data first.
  • Participant consent model — individuals opt in directly.
  • Private ADHD clinical partner route — continuity around a private pathway.
  • Right to Choose support route — continuity around RtC-driven assessment.

The mission is the constant; the route is the variable. OTOS follows the person, not the silo.

This is not unprecedented on the addiction side.

Change Grow Live has already published practice-development work — through Nottingham CVS's Practice Development Unit — on psychotherapeutic and diagnostic assessment approaches for ADHD within substance-use services, mapping the real barriers in this cohort: symptom overlap, substance-induced symptoms, stigma and misdiagnosis, missed appointments, chaotic lifestyle. It is not a pathway or a service — it is evidence that ADHD is already being recognised inside addiction settings. OTOS does not duplicate that clinical work; it makes continuity visible between the addiction and ADHD pathways, so the cohort, once identified, is fast-tracked and held rather than lost.

Source · CGL / Nottingham CVS Practice Development Unit — “Psychotherapeutic assessment approaches in the diagnostic assessment of ADHD.”
