The sequence that mattered

Therapy worked — once the brain had what it needed.

The therapy, the CBT, the recovery work — none of it was wasted. It started to hold the moment the ADHD underneath was finally being treated. This is the cohort OTOS is built around: adults whose addiction may be ADHD-driven.

I came through the Mind-funded WorkWell East route on a GP referral. I was given options, and I chose therapy. It was genuinely good work — and the reason I am writing is not to praise it in passing, but to point at the one thing that made it land: sequence.

For years, support like this could not reach me. Once my ADHD was being properly treated, the same kind of work suddenly went in. That is the whole thesis of what I am building — and Mind, WorkWell and therapy are central to it, not peripheral. This is a private note, not a pitch, and assumes no endorsement.

My live position

I was being helped — but not yet joined up.

Here is exactly where I sit today, told plainly and without blame. I am privately diagnosed and medicated through Psymplicity. My GP has not been able to accept shared care because of the route to that diagnosis — I did not come through Right to Choose — so I am now waiting for the CPFT Adult ADHD Team to progress the NHS pathway with my new psychiatric letter. That letter is going to them now.

In the meantime, the system did not leave me with nothing. My GP referred me to WorkWell East, which came through Mind. I could choose from several kinds of support, and I chose therapy. It helped — genuinely. But it sat beside an unresolved ADHD route, not joined to it.

“A person can be supported and still not joined up.” That gap — between being helped and being held — is exactly the space OTOS is built to hold.

This is not a failure of Mind, WorkWell or my GP. They each did their part. The problem is that the parts were never threaded together — and known to services is not the same as held between services.

The therapy room

Where the story could finally be spoken in order.

Therapy content stays private — nothing said in the room is part of OTOS

Therapy, with Jo Fenton, sat independent at the end of that route. The content stayed in the room, as it should — and that boundary is exactly why it worked. Week by week, I brought my life story as I built OTOS: a steady rhythm of reflection and containment that let me organise something chaotic into something useful.

“Each week, I was not just attending therapy. I was building OTOS out loud.” Therapy was the place the founder story slowly became the product story.

And from her chair, Jo has seen something the data is only beginning to show: more and more people arriving who suspect they have ADHD, who are waiting for assessment, waiting for medication, or struggling at the sharp end of its impact. The therapy room is one of the places where the hidden ADHD wave is already arriving.

Jo is referenced only with consent — and as no endorsement. Nothing here is written in her name, and no therapy content, notes or disclosures are part of OTOS. This page simply records what the therapy route made possible — and what therapists are quietly witnessing.

The VCSE wedge

Charities shouldn't need patient-record access to contribute.

There is a practical system-design problem here. NHS single-patient-record models can struggle to include VCSE, charity and independent-therapy partners, because they may not sit inside the same data and clinical-record environment. The result is that some of the most human support a person receives stays invisible to the wider pathway.

OTOS closes that gap from the other direction — with consent-led, minimal continuity signals. No therapy content. No EPR write-back. No new clinical record. No NHS record access.

A team of services · the ask

Therapy can stay sacrosanct. The thread sits beside it.

I am not asking any one organisation to become the solution. I am trying to build a team of services around the person — the ADHD Team for the clinical pathway, CGL and CRS for recovery-side visibility, Alcohol Liaison for upstream interception, RCE for active waiting, and Mind / WorkWell / therapy for mental-health, employment and reflective support. OTOS is the non-clinical thread that sits between them.

  • It helps the ADHD Team: support is visible and active while people wait, instead of a silent gap.
  • It helps CGL & CRS: people have somewhere safe and human to be between recovery conversations.
  • It helps the person: the support they were sent towards does not vanish onto a disconnected island.
The ask

If appropriate, I would value a 30–45 minute conversation about whether Mind / WorkWell / therapy support could help shape the VCSE and therapy continuity layer inside a small, safe, bounded ADHD-driven addiction continuity-learning model. No endorsement. No commitment. Just your practical mental-health, therapy and VCSE judgement in the room.

Boundary, named

OTOS does not diagnose, prescribe, treat, replace services or make clinical decisions. OTOS surfaces signals; partner teams make the decisions. No therapy content, notes or disclosures are ever part of OTOS; no EPR write-back; no NHS record access. No endorsement is assumed or implied. Any work would be consent-led and subject to governance.
