The waiting layer

Not passive waiting. Active continuity.

For this cohort, the advice is almost always the same: wait. Wait for the assessment, the titration, the review, the next appointment. But waiting without structure is exactly where ADHD-driven drift becomes addiction-driven crisis. A person can be clinically known and still operationally lost.

RCE is one of the few places that already turns that empty wait into something real — courses, recovery education, routine and organisation support, breathwork, meditation, coping tools, diagnosis education, community-style self-management. It gives the person something to engage with while the clinical pathway catches up.

OTOS wraps a continuity thread around that, so the waiting period is no longer a silence the system can't see. Known to services is not the same as held between services — and RCE is one of the best existing places that thread can point.

RCE gives the person something real to engage with while the clinical pathway catches up. OTOS keeps the thread alive around it. RCE is not a signpost — RCE is part of what holds the person.

The moment it lands

This is the moment the work can finally go in.

This is the part I most want RCE to hear, because I lived it. Before the ADHD was recognised and treated, the support could not land. I could not turn my laptop on. I could not sequence a course, hold a routine, or absorb the recovery work — and I had been through a lot of it.

Once the treatment was right, everything changed. The CBT started to hold. The recovery learning landed. RCE's courses suddenly gave me structure and knowledge I could actually use. The work had always been good — my brain just hadn't been able to take it in.

“Medication does not do the recovery work. But it creates the conditions where the work can finally land.” — and RCE is exactly the kind of support that should be waiting at that moment.

That is why the newly diagnosed and newly medicated moment matters so much. It is a narrow, precious window where CBT, routine building, self-management, emotional regulation, addiction education, ADHD education and practical wellbeing can finally become usable. RCE is built for exactly that window.

What we would need from RCE

Be exactly what you already are — with a thread around it.

None of this asks RCE to change what it does or take on anything clinical. The whole point is to use RCE's existing strengths, with continuity wrapped gently around them. Nothing changes in how you work. What changes is what can be seen.

A short scoping call

What we'd explore together.

  • A named contact, and a 30–45 minute conversation.
  • Which courses or sessions could work as continuity next steps.
  • What language feels safe and right in RCE's voice.
  • Which interaction signals would be appropriate to log.
  • How RCE wants its boundaries held.
What we would never do

The boundaries, named.

  • No clinical records replaced; no EPR write-back.
  • No asking RCE to diagnose, treat or manage medication.
  • No automated clinical decisions from a reply or a silence.
  • No commitment to rollout — learning, not claiming.
RCE is not being asked to become a clinical decision-maker. It is being asked to be exactly what it already is — a real, trusted next step — with a thread around it so the person doesn't disappear while they wait.

The comparison, item for item

What recovery needs. What ADHD takes away.

This is why the newly-medicated window matters so much to RCE.

Recovery and learning ask for focus, memory, routine and emotional regulation. Unmanaged ADHD weakens all four. OTOS tests whether treating the driver and holding the thread makes the same courses — the ones that could not land before — finally usable.

The full capacity-by-capacity comparison is set out in the OTOS Evidence Dossier. Read it as a model, not a verdict — presentations vary and the alignment is directional.

The science of sticking. NICE recommends medication and structured CBT in combination — and in adult studies, medication-plus-CBT outperforms either alone. The medication doesn't do the recovery work; it creates the conditions where it can finally land. Full evidence in the OTOS Evidence Dossier.

To be completely clear

What OTOS is, and what it is not.

Before any conversation, the boundary should be unambiguous. This protects RCE, your facilitators and your governance — OTOS adds a thread, never a clinical responsibility.

× What OTOS is not
  • × A clinical service, diagnosis tool or treatment
  • × An EPR or NHS record — no record access, no write-back
  • × A replacement for RCE, LAMP or any existing course
  • × Asking facilitators to take on anything clinical

✓ What OTOS is
  • ✓ A non-clinical continuity layer around services that already exist

Why your involvement matters · the ask

RCE can help turn waiting into something that holds.

Done well, RCE stops being a forgotten signpost at the end of a referral and becomes a visible, valued part of the pathway — the place that demonstrates how wellbeing education actually supports people while they wait, are newly diagnosed, or are newly medicated.

  • It helps the ADHD Team: “go and wait” becomes “go and engage with something structured while we keep the thread alive.”
  • It helps CGL & CRS: people have a real next step between recovery conversations.
  • It helps RCE: clearer feedback on who engaged, drifted, completed or needed follow-up — and proof of RCE's value inside a wider pathway.
The ask

I'm not asking you to approve OTOS, endorse NIA, commit to a pilot or take on new responsibility. I would value a 30–45 minute conversation about whether RCE could help shape the active waiting and wellbeing-support layer inside a small, safe, bounded ADHD-driven addiction continuity-learning model. Just your practical wellbeing and education judgement in the room.

Boundary, named

OTOS does not diagnose, prescribe, treat, replace services or make clinical decisions. OTOS surfaces signals; partner teams make the decisions. RCE is not a clinical service and is not asked to become one; RCE content stays in RCE's own voice and boundaries. No endorsement is assumed or implied. Any work would be RCE-shaped, consent-led and subject to governance.
