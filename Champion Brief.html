OTOS Life The story · 02 / 06
Why this exists

The chaos wasn't
a character defect.

Before OTOS Life had a name, I was the person it is being built for.

For most of my adult life I was stuck in the overlap between ADHD, addiction, self-medication and a long, quiet drift through services that were not designed to hold me. I was on lists. I was discharged between services. I was visible in the wrong rooms and invisible in the right ones. I lost a lot. People who love me lost a lot.

What changed wasn't willpower. What changed was that ADHD was finally properly identified and treated. That moment did not fix everything. It made everything else possible. I did not recover by waiting my turn.

The continuity I needed didn't exist. So I am building it.

OTOS Life exists because the gap I fell through is still there. OTOS Continuity is the first piece of it — a quiet, non-clinical layer designed to hold the line between appointments, between services, between waiting and being held.

That is the whole reason for this letter — and for the conversation it is asking for.

OTOS Life Why a champion might matter · 03 / 06
Why a champion might matter

Where the story is,
and isn't, being told.

ADHD has more cultural air than it used to. Addiction has its own public story. But the overlap — where unrecognised or under-treated ADHD drives self-medication, then addiction, then crisis — is still mostly invisible. The people inside that overlap rarely get to tell the story while they're still in it. The people outside it rarely tell it accurately. Known to services is not the same as held between services.

This is the gap a public champion can close — not by carrying a logo, but by helping the wider story be told honestly, with the right boundaries, at the right moment.

That is not a small thing to ask of anyone. So we are asking it carefully.

What “champion” can mean here.

  • It can mean private feedback on the work, full stop.
  • It can mean an introduction to one person who would matter.
  • It can mean an advisory conversation, off the record, as the work develops.
  • It can mean, much later, and only if it ever feels right, a piece of public support — on terms you set, on a moment you choose.
  • It can mean alignment with a foundation, charity or platform you already lead, if that is relevant.
This is not a request for blind endorsement. We are not asking you to publicly back a project you have only just heard of. We are asking for the start of a careful conversation. The public story supports the work; partner teams still make every clinical decision.

The invitation

Five ways this could go,
in your own time.

There is no expected answer to this brief. There is no “right” level of involvement. The five levels below are a menu, not a ladder. Any of them — including the first — would matter.

01

Private feedback.

Read the brief. Tell us, privately, what lands and what doesn't. That alone is valuable.

02

An introduction.

If there is a single person in your circle this would matter to — a clinician, a foundation lead, an advocate, a peer — a careful intro from you would open a door we cannot open alone.

03

An advisory conversation.

A small number of off-the-record conversations as the work develops. No public association. No commitment.

04

Public support, only if and when appropriate.

Much later, and only if the work earns it on its own terms, a piece of public support — on a moment and in a form you would choose entirely.

05

Foundation or charity alignment.

If you already lead, fund or advise a foundation, charity or platform whose mission this sits inside, alignment at that level could be explored quietly.

What we are not asking for

  • A name to use.
  • A quote we can publish.
  • A social post.
  • An endorsement of early-stage work.
  • Public association OTOS has not yet earned.
  • A reply that commits you to anything.
Your reputation, your lived experience and your consent are the constants here. Nothing in this brief is shared beyond a small private circle. Nothing about you would be referenced publicly without your written agreement, on a moment you choose. If at any point this is not for you, a single line back saying so is all that is needed.

What I'd love next

A quiet conversation.
That is the whole ask.

If anything in this brief stayed with you, what I'd love is a single conversation. Thirty minutes. Off the record. No deck, no media, no team. Just you and me, with whoever you want in the room with you.

If after that conversation the answer is “thank you, but no”, that is a complete answer. If the answer is “tell me when there is more to see”, that is also a complete answer. If the answer is “let me think about how I could help”, we will design what that means together, slowly, on your terms.

The thing I will not do is treat your reply as the start of a chase. That is not what this is.

Why now

The gap between ADHD recognition and addiction recovery is wider than the public conversation suggests. People are being lost inside it now. OTOS is being built carefully, slowly, and with clinical boundaries respected. The earliest conversations shape what this becomes.

Confidentiality

This brief is private. It will not be circulated, referenced or attributed without your written agreement. Anything you tell me in the conversation that follows is yours to decide what we do with.

The reply. If a 30-minute private conversation feels possible, a one-line reply with a couple of windows that work for you is all I need. If not, a one-line “not for me” is equally welcome and equally respected. Nothing public ever happens without your separate, written consent and timing you choose.
